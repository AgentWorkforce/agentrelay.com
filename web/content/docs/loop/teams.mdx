For an individual, Relayloop is a searchable memory. For a team, it becomes shared organizational memory — and an improvement loop that raises everyone's baseline. The thesis behind [Relayloop](/docs/loop/introduction) is that AI leverage is bimodal: your best engineers get 5–10× from coding agents while most of the team gets 1×, and the difference is prompting skill that today is tribal and undocumented. Team features close that gap and make sure nothing evaporates when people leave.

## Shared organizational memory

When teammates opt into [cloud](/docs/loop/cloud) sync and share sessions, the team's prompts become a common corpus. Anyone can search how a colleague approached a problem instead of solving it from scratch. Your best engineers' patterns become a living playbook the whole team draws from — the Recall lens, but team-wide.

## Onboarding acceleration

New hires inherit the team's accumulated AI history on day one. Instead of learning the codebase and the team's prompting style by osmosis, they can search how the team has approached similar work and start from a known-good pattern. The ramp gets shorter and first-try acceptance goes up.

## Knowledge retention

When someone leaves, their AI-assisted work history stays with the organization. The sessions, the decisions, and — through trajectory records — the *why* behind them, all remain searchable. Institutional knowledge that used to walk out the door now stays.

## The network that learns

The Insights flywheel is what a single-team tool cannot match. Relayloop does not only learn from your team — it learns what works across every team on the platform and feeds the generalized, anonymized lessons back to you.

> The more teams on Relayloop, the smarter your engineers' agents get.

Your engineers get the benefit of patterns proven across thousands of others, automatically, without you running the experiments. Cross-team learning is always aggregated and anonymized — one customer never sees another's content — and your data is never used to train models without your explicit opt-in. Patterns turn into proactive guidance and repo rules, so your baseline rises every week and your codebase gets steadily more agent-ready. Teams that need full isolation can run the Enterprise tier end-to-end encrypted or in their own VPC, trading the flywheel for total privacy. See [Privacy](/docs/loop/privacy) for the trust model.

## What to expect

- Faster ramp for new and junior engineers.
- Higher first-try acceptance and less agent rework.
- Lower, more predictable token spend ([Burn](/docs/loop/stats)).
- A prompting playbook that improves on its own — and gets better as the network grows.
